# The Relationship Between Menstrual Cycles & Daily Calorie Intake 

## Definitions 
**BMR** (Basal Metabolic Rate) => amount of calories the body burns while resting 

**TDEE** (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) => BMR + activity level = your baseline (what the app will update based on the user's current phase)


## Menstruation (Phase 1)
- When the uterus lining is shedding 
- lasts for 3 to 7 days
- low estrogen & progesterone 
- CALORIE INTAKE: stay at baseline
- in this phase, its more important to listen to your body 
- iron and comfort foods are important
- due to hormonal fluctuations, your body will be hungrier

## Follicular (Phase 2) 
- starts on the first day of your period
- lasts for 13 to 14 days 
- estrogen and energy goes up 
- CALORIE INTAKE: around or less than daily baseline 
- should be hitting deficit goals 

## Ovulation (Phase 3) 
- happens about 2 weeks before your next period 
- estrogen peaks
- CALORIE INTAKE: hit daily baseline (but your appetite decreases)

## Luteal (Phase 4)
- starts in days 17 to 28 of your cycle 
- progesterone rises and BMR increases 
- CALORIE INTAKE: typically 150 to 300 more calories is needed 
- due to progesterone rising, it raises the metabolic rate causing cravings to be stronger before a period
